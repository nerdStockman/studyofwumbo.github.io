Our Mission

The full mission and vision behind H.A.W.K.

The H.A.W.K Flight Foundation was created to empower youth and young adults through aviation education, mentorship, and career opportunity.

🛫

Mission Statement

At Hancock Aviation Workforce Knowledge (H.A.W.K.) Flight Foundation, our mission is to empower the youth and young adults of Hancock County, particularly those from low-income backgrounds, by providing comprehensive funding and support for training programs in aviation. We are dedicated to developing skilled professionals in the fields of piloting, aircraft mechanics, and airport operations. Through education, mentorship, and community partnerships, H.A.W.K. strives to create pathways to rewarding careers in aviation, fostering economic growth, and enriching our community.

A community-centered nonprofit focused on aviation opportunity.
Foundation Goal

Accessible, high-quality aviation training for future aviators.

The foundation’s primary goal is to foster a skilled aviation workforce in Hancock County by making training opportunities accessible to low-income youth and young adults.

✓ Provide scholarships and hands-on training pathways.
✓ Support careers in flight, maintenance, and airport operations.
✓ Build partnerships that lead to employment and long-term economic advancement.
Programs

Career pathways across the aviation ecosystem.

H.A.W.K can grow into a hub for training partnerships, mentorship, scholarships, and job placement.

Pilot Training

Scholarship and program support for students pursuing flight training, certifications, and hands-on aviation experience.

Aircraft Maintenance

Funding and partnerships for students entering technical programs that lead to careers maintaining and repairing aircraft.

Airport Operations

Exposure, training, and placement support for roles that keep airports, airlines, and aviation businesses moving safely.

Strategic Objectives

A practical plan for measurable impact.

These objectives translate the foundation’s mission into action for students, families, partners, and the broader aviation community.

1
Expand Educational Programs Develop and implement a range of training programs through community partnerships that cover all aspects of aviation careers, including piloting, mechanics, and airport operations, tailored specifically to meet the needs of Hancock County’s youth and young adults.
2
Increase Scholarship Opportunities Secure funding to provide scholarships that cover the full cost of training for eligible low-income students, ensuring financial barriers do not prevent talented individuals from pursuing aviation careers.
3
Purchase an Economical Aircraft Acquire a cost-effective, reliable aircraft to facilitate hands-on pilot training. This investment will enable practical learning experiences, enhancing the quality of training provided.
4
Strengthen Community Partnerships Forge and maintain partnerships with local businesses, educational institutions, and government entities to support training programs, provide employment opportunities, and ensure long-term sustainability.
5
Promote Career Placement Establish a robust career placement program that assists graduates in finding employment within the aviation industry immediately upon completion of their training.
6
Enhance Outreach Efforts Conduct outreach activities to raise awareness about aviation career opportunities available through H.A.W.K., focusing on engaging schools, community centers, and local media.
7
Monitor and Evaluate Impact Regularly assess the effectiveness of training programs and adjust strategies as necessary to maximize impact on students’ career readiness and success in the aviation industry.
